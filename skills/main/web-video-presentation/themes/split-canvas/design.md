## Design Philosophy
Split Canvas makes the stage itself the design statement — a dual-tone split with warm peach on the left and cool lavender on the right. This is NOT a background color; it is the primary compositional tool. Chapters freely choose which side to land on: a thesis on the left, an antithesis on the right; before/after; problem/solution; two perspectives in dialog. The rose pink accent (#d24a78) bridges both halves — it has enough warmth for the peach side, enough coolness for the lavender side. Outfit carries the typographic voice with its clean geometric character — friendly without being informal, structured without being rigid. This theme is for comparison content, debate/dialog formats, creative portfolios, light brand films, and concept explainers. It is NOT for single-topic deep dives, formal corporate presentations, academic papers, or data-dense reports.

## Colors
The palette is built on a dual-tone foundation with a single bridging accent. The stage is split: left half surface (#f5e6dc) warm peach, right half lavender (#e8e0f0). Shell (#dcc7b8) is a darker peach — used for navigation chrome, footer zones, and transitional elements that span both halves. Surface-2 (#ead8ca) provides a tertiary warm plane when needed. Text (#1a1410) is warm near-black — neutral enough to read on both peach and lavender, with a subtle warmth that harmonizes with both halves. Text-2 (#3a2a20) is warmer brown for secondary copy. Text-mute (#7a6258) and text-faint (#a89888) descend through warm earth tones. The accent (#d24a78) is rose pink — it bridges the warm/cool divide, carrying just enough magenta coolness to work on the lavender side and just enough red warmth to work on the peach side. It touches hero numbers, section markers, and focal emphasis. The lavender half is a stage element only — it never appears in rules, cards, or text.

## Typography
Outfit at 700 weight carries the voice — a clean geometric sans-serif with friendly proportions and open apertures that works equally well on both halves of the stage. Outfit at 400 weight handles body copy — the single-typeface approach creates visual unity across the split stage, letting the dual-tone background do the compositional work. JetBrains Mono at 500 serves technical content. CJK uses Noto Sans SC at 700 for display, matching Outfit's geometric character. Maximum two typefaces per slide. The typographic system is intentionally neutral — the stage split is the personality; the type should not compete with it.

## Layout & Components
The stage is a hard 50/50 vertical split: warm peach (#f5e6dc) on the left, cool lavender (#e8e0f0) on the right. No gradient, no transition — the hard edge IS the design. Content can land entirely on one side, centered on the split line, or distributed across both halves depending on the narrative purpose. Cards are warm white (surface) — they work on either half of the stage and never take on the half's color. Rules are 1px warm peach (#ccb7a8) — understated, structural, neutral enough for either half. Hero numbers are Outfit at 700 in rose pink, tabular figures, 72-100px — they bridge both halves chromatically. Navigation chrome and stage footer use shell (#dcc7b8). The split position may shift (60/40, 70/30) for specific layouts but must remain consistent within a chapter.

## Do's
1. Maintain the 50/50 stage split as the default — it is the theme's identity. Shift ratios only with intention.
2. Choose which side content lands on based on narrative purpose — left for thesis/tradition, right for antithesis/innovation.
3. Use the rose pink accent (#d24a78) as the single bridging element — it works on both halves.
4. Keep cards warm white — they should work on either half without taking on the half's color.
5. Use Outfit for all text — typographic unity lets the stage split do the compositional work.
6. Maintain a hard edge between halves — never use a gradient transition.
7. Use the split for meaningful narrative contrast — not just decoration.

## Don'ts
1. Never blend the two halves with a gradient — the hard edge is the design statement.
2. Never use the lavender color outside the stage background — it is a stage element only.
3. Never use different typography on different halves — the unity of Outfit across the split is essential.
4. Never place critical content directly on the split line at body sizes — the hard color change disrupts readability.
5. Never use more than one accent color — rose pink is the complete accent palette.
6. Never use dark or aggressive colors — the dual-tone stage is light, warm, and inviting.
7. Never abandon the split entirely for more than a title card or section divider.

## CJK Notes
Noto Sans SC at 700 weight for display and 400 weight for body matches Outfit's clean geometric character. CJK line-height minimum 1.75. The dual-tone stage split works identically across scripts — position CJK content following the same narrative logic as Latin content.

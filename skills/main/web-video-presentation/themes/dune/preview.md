## Visual Snapshot
A vast expanse of desert-sand beige serves as the canvas, its warmth so uniform it feels like unprimed linen or a gallery wall at midday. Charcoal-brown text in Inter sits with extraordinarily wide padding — each element given room to breathe, nothing crowding anything else. The accent color barely appears at all; when it does, it is a single thin rule or the faintest tint on one numeral. What dominates is absence: no bold, no saturated color, no ornament — just the confidence of negative space and the quiet authority of brown ink on warm paper.

## Design Philosophy
Vintage Editorial channels the voice of a magazine columnist who knows you'll laugh — witty, conversational, and full of personality. The visual language sits between a literary magazine and an opinion column: warm off-white paper (#e8e0cf), terracotta accent (#c25e3a), and Fraunces italic as the distinctive typographic voice. The signature decorative element is a geometric line overlay — thin circles, lines, and dots in the accent color at low opacity, scattered across the stage like margin doodles or abstract editorial illustrations. This is NOT cold, NOT corporate, NOT academic-formal. It is opinionated, warm, and slightly irreverent. The theme is for personal essays/commentary, cultural criticism, design/font topics, literary content, and any video where the presenter's voice is the primary draw. It is NOT for formal business, academic papers, legal/compliance, or data-dense reports.

## Colors
The palette is warm paper with a single terracotta accent. Shell (#e8e0cf) is warm off-white — the paper of a well-read magazine, slightly yellowed with age and personality. Surface (#f5f3ee) is lighter warm white for cards and elevated planes. Surface-2 (#ece7d8) provides a mid-tone for nested elements. Text (#1a1a1a) is near-black — neutral enough for extended reading but warm enough to harmonize with the paper. Text-2 (#3a3a3a) is dark gray for secondary copy. Text-mute (#7a7a7a) and text-faint (#aaaaaa) descend through neutral grays. The accent (#c25e3a) is terracotta — warm, earthy, with enough red to feel opinionated and enough brown to feel grounded. It touches hero numbers, rules, the geometric overlay, and section markers. Accent-soft (10%) and accent-glow (18%) are deliberately restrained — the accent is warm, not hot. The rule IS the accent color — there is no separate rule tone.

## Typography
Fraunces italic at 400 weight is the voice — a chunky, humanist serif in italic that feels personal, opinionated, and slightly unconventional. The italic is structural — it IS the typeface identity, carrying the columnist's byline energy. Work Sans at 400 weight handles body copy — a clean, friendly sans-serif with open apertures that provides neutral support without competing with Fraunces. The Fraunces/Work Sans pairing creates the essential tension: expressive italic serif voice + clean sans-serif substance. IBM Plex Mono at 500 serves technical content. CJK uses Noto Serif SC at 700 for display, matching Fraunces's warm literary character. The typographic system supports the conversational, witty tone — Fraunces italic delivers the personality; Work Sans delivers the substance.

## Layout & Components
The stage is warm off-white paper with a geometric line overlay — thin (1px) line-art circles, lines, and dots in terracotta at 8% opacity, abstractly scattered across the stage like margin doodles. The overlay is the signature decoration and should appear on most slides (composition may vary but should remain abstract, never forming recognizable illustrations). Cards are warm white (surface) with subtle shadows — refined and understated, letting the geometric overlay carry the visual personality. Rules are 1px terracotta lines — the same color as the accent, warm and opinionated. Hero numbers are Fraunces italic in terracotta, tabular figures, 72-100px — witty and opinionated, never loud. Density is medium — room for editorial asides, pull quotes, and personality elements.

## Do's
1. Use Fraunces italic for all English display text — the italic IS the columnist's voice.
2. Apply the geometric line overlay on most slides — it is the signature personality element.
3. Use terracotta (#c25e3a) for rules, hero numbers, section markers, and the geometric overlay.
4. Pair Fraunces italic display with Work Sans body — the expressive/neutral contrast is the signature.
5. Keep the geometric overlay abstract — circles, lines, dots, never forming literal illustrations.
6. Maintain a conversational, witty tone in the content — the visual and verbal personality must match.
7. Vary the geometric overlay composition across slides — it should feel like improvised margin doodles.

## Don'ts
1. Never use Fraunces for body copy — it is a display typeface and fatigues at reading sizes.
2. Never fill a card with the terracotta accent — it is for type, rules, and the overlay, not surfaces.
3. Never use cold grays, blues, or cool tones — the palette is warm paper + terracotta only.
4. Never make the geometric overlay too dense or regular — it should feel improvised, not systematic.
5. Never use the terracotta for extended body text — it is an accent, not an ink color.
6. Never use bold sans-serif for English display — Fraunces italic is the complete display vocabulary.
7. Never use geometric sans-serif for body — Work Sans's humanist warmth is essential.

## CJK Notes
Noto Serif SC at 700 weight for display and 400 weight for body carries the warm literary character across scripts. CJK line-height minimum 1.75. The geometric overlay works identically across scripts — it is a graphic element, not typographic. CJK has no italic; the Fraunces italic personality cannot be directly replicated, but Noto Serif SC at 700 provides sufficient literary presence.
